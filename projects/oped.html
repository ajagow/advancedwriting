The Problems with Electronic Health Records.

op-ed
Summary

A public writing piece that follows an op-ed format, similar to what you'd find in an online magazine, like WIRED, this piece demonstrates my ability to write non-scholarly articles.

In this piece, I discuss different grey-area data selling practices found in the Medical Data market that have cropped up as a result of Electornic Health Records. Many people don't know that their medical data gets sold to big corporations in a mulit-billionare dollar industry without their knowledge or permission.

This kind of data mining is completely legal, and falls under a category that's outside the purview of medical privacy acts like the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA).

Thoughts

This type of writing really let me be creative and explore writing in a more casual tone. I had to make sure I thoroughly explained background concepts in an easy to understand manner and lay out my arguments well.
