Electronic Health Records Analysis.

research
Summary

A research paper outlining the history, benefits, and drawbacks of Electronic Health Records (EHRs). This paper was written in collaboration with Erik Jaklitsch and Ann Kogosov, two pre-med students.

We explored the benefits of EHRs, such as better access to medical data for patients and increases in physician productivity. Then, we discussed the drawbacks associated with EHRs, such as physician burnout and out-dated technology, and evaluated solutions to these problems. Our proposal included ideas like implementing scribe programs so that doctors don't have to write information down during appointments, and integrating new technologies, like hive, to handle the increase demands in data access and storage.

Our resources included various peer reviewed journal articles.

Thoughts

I really enjoyed this project because it forced me to communicate proactively and effectively with my peers. Additionally, I had to learn how to meld my writing styling with two other people's. Through frequent communication, and a lot of group editing and team work, we were all able to create a cohesive paper that had a unified voice.

Additionally, I learned how to find reliable sources, like peer reviewed journal articles, and evaluate them. There are a lot of articles and information out on the internet that pretend to be legitimate, but actually share very bias and skewed data.
