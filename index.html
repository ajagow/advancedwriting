about me.

Hi! I'm a third year Computer Science and Digital Art major at Northeastern University in Boston, MA. I first got interested in Computer Science after attending a summer camp where I learned how to program in Java. After coming to college, I realized that I wanted to combine my two passions, coding and art, so I decided to pursue both.

about this professional writing portfolio.

I've had an interest in writing since I was young. Even now, I love reading articles online about current events and modern-day moral issues. I find ethical dilemmas that arise because of advancements in technology fascinating. With my friends, I love debating about the ethics of self driving cars, artificially intelligent machines, and other moral dilemmas that have come about in recent years because of new technology.

selected works.

In my Advanced Writing class, we had the opportunity to research topics within our discipline that connected with other disciplines, and work with students from different majors to find solutions to these interdisciplinary problems.

We worked on two major research pieces that were intended for different audiences: scholarly and the public. Making this distinction forced me to think about who would be reading each piece, and how my writing style should reflect the change in intended audience.

The first piece is a collaborative research paper written with two of my peers who are pre-med. We explored the topic of Electronic Health Records and suggested ways in which we can improve the current system.

The second writing piece, a public writing sample, is an op-ed about privacy issues that are happening as a result of Electronic Health Records and what we, as consumers, need to do to put an end to these grey-area practices.
